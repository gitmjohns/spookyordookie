# SpookyorDookie Master Content Policy & Approved Title List

## IMPORTANT NOTES FOR CLAUDE CODE
- Movies can and should have MULTIPLE genre tags
- Italian Horror and Cult Classics are NOT user-facing genre filter options
- Italian Horror titles should be tagged under their actual genres: Supernatural, Slasher, Body Horror, Psychological, etc.
- Cult Classics is an internal tag only - used for the homepage row, not exposed as a sort/filter option
- Every title in the database must have at least one genre tag and a critic score
- No animation of any kind - movies or TV
- Minimum year 1968 for movies. Only exceptions: Night of the Living Dead 1968 (categorize under 1970s decade) and Rosemary's Baby 1968

## RATING SCALE
- 90-100: Certified Spooky
- 75-89: Full Spooks
- 60-74: Slight Spook
- 45-59: Meh-diocre
- 30-44: Slight Dook
- 15-29: Full Dookie
- 0-14: Steaming Dookie

## CRITIC SCORE GUIDELINES
- Iconic 80s and 90s classics: 85-98
- Early 2000s horror: 75-88
- 70s classics: 80-92
- 2010s horror: 65-82
- Recent releases 2020s: 50-70 unless exceptional
- Divisive cult films: 60-80
- Genuinely bad films: 5-30
- Every single title must have a critic score - no exceptions

## CONTENT RULES

### What Belongs
- Horror films and TV from 1968 to present
- Sci-Fi Horror if it leans heavily into horror elements
- Horror adjacent if it has genuine supernatural elements: monsters, creatures, hostile ghosts, spirits, supernatural killers, body horror
- European horror with Western recognition
- Asian horror only from the approved list
- New exceptional releases from anywhere in the world

### What Does NOT Belong
- Anything before 1968 except Night of the Living Dead 1968 and Rosemary's Baby 1968
- Animation of any kind
- Dystopian dramas without supernatural elements
- Political thrillers without horror elements
- Crime dramas without supernatural elements
- Sci-fi action without genuine horror elements
- Obscure foreign films without Western recognition
- Anime

### Geographic Priority
1. North American horror (primary)
2. European horror - UK, Italian, French, Spanish, German, Scandinavian (secondary)
3. Asian horror - approved titles only (selective)
4. Exceptional new releases from anywhere (case by case)
